Justice Policy

Background

Aotearoa’s current justice system is rooted in colonial control and systemic inequality. Māori make up over 50% of the prison population despite being just 17% of the general population. Prisons perpetuate cycles of trauma, violence, and intergenerational harm without addressing root causes like poverty, colonisation, mental illness, and addiction. Billions are spent maintaining a punitive system that fails to deliver safety, rehabilitation, or justice.

Vision

We believe in a justice system that heals rather than harms — one that restores balance, upholds mana, and addresses the social roots of harm. Our vision is a future without prisons, where justice is relational, transformative, and led by the communities most affected by harm.

Objectives

  • End mass incarceration and dismantle the prison-industrial complex.
  • Restore Māori leadership and tikanga in justice processes.
  • Invest in housing, mental health, addiction support, and community-led solutions to reduce harm.
  • Shift from punitive to restorative and transformative justice models.

Policy Commitments

  • Set a national target to close all prisons by 2040, with a phased approach toward de-incarceration.
  • Fund kaupapa Māori justice systems such as whānau courts, rangatahi courts, and iwi-based restorative models.
  • Legalise and regulate cannabis, including expungement of all past cannabis-related convictions.
  • Redirect justice sector funding into housing, health, and community wellbeing, reducing police and prison budgets year by year.
  • Expand restorative justice and harm prevention programmes that prioritise accountability, healing, and safety for all involved.

Implementation Plan

  • Establish a Commission for Transformative Justice to guide the transition toward a post-prison system in partnership with Māori, survivors of violence, and former prisoners.
  • Enact a Cannabis Regulation Act to legalise cannabis, prioritise Māori and Pasifika enterprise licences, and clear all minor drug convictions.
  • Pilot and expand community accountability hubs that replace courts and prisons with culturally grounded mediation, care, and prevention services.
  • Integrate mental health, housing, and addiction services into frontline responses to crime, reducing police involvement in non-violent incidents.
  • Create binding partnerships with iwi and hapū to lead justice processes within their rohe (regions), supported by public funding and statutory authority.

Timeframe

  • Year 1: Launch Transformative Justice Commission; begin cannabis legalisation process; shift 10% of prison and police funding to community programmes.
  • Year 2–5: Close private prisons; open 10+ community justice hubs; implement expungement of convictions.
  • Year 6–15: Gradual closure of all prisons, with reinvestment into housing, education, healing, and whānau-based justice alternatives.
  • By 2040: Achieve a prison-free Aotearoa grounded in whakapapa, restoration, and equity.
